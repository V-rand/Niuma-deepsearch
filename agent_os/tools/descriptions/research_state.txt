Research control and working-notes tool. Required for repeated short-answer retrieval; optional for long-form or complex retrieval when you need to track active goals, evidence targets, failed paths, or next actions.

It externalizes working state that should not live only in private reasoning and returns action cards for the next move:
- compact working notes: question_type, active_goal, current_action, known, unknown, failed_paths, evidence_target, exit_condition, next_move
- active constraint
- known facts already available
- reasoning paths from known facts
- candidates and their matched / failed / missing hard constraints
- evidence verdicts
- no-progress rounds and failed pivots

Typical short-answer flow:
1. start with question_model
2. focus_constraint before searching
3. inventory_known_facts before web_search when the constraint may be solved by reasoning
4. update_candidate / add_evidence after tools or reasoning
5. round_update after each research round
6. analyze_constraint when a clue needs reasoning rather than broad search
7. working_notes every few retrieval/reasoning steps, when stuck, or before pivot/answer. Use current_action to distinguish reason, lookup, match, search, verify, and answer.
8. next_action to get control signals before searching again

If next_action returns must_inventory_known_facts, first list what is already known about the active candidate/constraint and possible reasoning paths. For discovery-first paper/document lookup, a targeted high-entropy retrieval plan may be the right next action.

If action_card blocks web_search/web_read, follow the card first. It is designed to turn "think harder" into a concrete reasoning task.
